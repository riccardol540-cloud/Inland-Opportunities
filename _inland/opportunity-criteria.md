# Opportunity criteria — the Scout's rubric

*The Scout reads this before scanning. Its job is to surface fundable, relevant
opportunities and to filter out noise — not to apply. Use this to decide what gets a
brief in `Opportunities/inbox/` and how to score it.*

## What counts as an "opportunity"

Anything that could unlock or fund a future INLAND edition or body of work:

- **Public funding** — grants and open calls from EU programmes, national arts/culture
  councils, regional cultural funds, foundations.
- **Cultural institutes** — calls, residencies, or partnership openings from Goethe
  Institut, Institut français, British Council, and comparable national institutes, and
  from private foundations.
- **Partnerships / co-productions** — NGOs, universities, museums, festivals, or local
  institutions in a target territory open to collaboration or commissioning.

(These three channels are the agreed scope. Photography/documentary residencies and
journalism grants are in scope when they fit the editorial profile below.)

## Hard filters (drop if any fail)

- **Eligibility**: an independent publisher / small cultural producer based in Italy /
  the EU can actually apply or partner. Drop if restricted to other regions or to
  institutions INLAND is not.
- **Live**: deadline is in the future as of the scan date (or rolling). Flag anything
  closing within 21 days as URGENT.
- **Purpose fit**: supports cultural documentation, publishing, photography,
  documentary, heritage, or place-based storytelling. Drop pure tech/STEM, pure
  commercial, or unrelated calls.

## Fit score (1–5) — how well it matches INLAND

Score on editorial + practical fit, and record a one-line rationale:

- **5 — Bullseye**: funds a place-based documentation project about living traditions,
  minorities, heritage, or cultural continuity in a specific territory; INLAND's format
  is a natural fit; meaningful budget.
- **4 — Strong**: clearly relevant theme (culture, heritage, photography, publishing,
  cross-border), applicable, decent budget; minor reframing needed.
- **3 — Possible**: adjacent (e.g. general arts grant, broad media fund) that could fit
  with a tailored angle.
- **2 — Weak**: eligible but a stretch thematically or very small budget.
- **1 — Marginal**: keep only if nothing better; likely archive.

Surface 3–5; mention 2 only if the week is thin; do not write briefs for 1s.

## Signals that raise a score

- Names a **territory** with rich, under-documented living traditions (good candidate
  for a future issue).
- Explicitly funds **photography, documentary, publishing, oral history, or intangible
  cultural heritage**.
- Multi-year or production-scale budget (covers field embedding and print).
- A **cultural institute or embassy** tied to a specific country (mirrors the Kashmir
  embassy-collaborator model).
- Cross-border or minority-culture focus.

## Signals that lower a score

- Academic-only outputs, no public-facing cultural product.
- Requires a large consortium or co-financing INLAND can't realistically raise.
- Tight deadline with heavy bespoke application burden relative to budget.
- Region or theme far from INLAND's profile.

## What each brief must capture

When the Scout writes a brief into `Opportunities/inbox/`, include:

- **Title** and **funder/host**
- **Channel** (public funding / cultural institute / partnership)
- **Link**
- **Deadline** (and URGENT flag if < 21 days)
- **Budget / award size**
- **Eligibility** (one line — why INLAND qualifies)
- **Fit score (1–5)** + **one-line rationale**
- **Possible territory / angle** for an INLAND edition
- **Required application documents** (so the Producer knows what to build)
- **Status**: `new` (default), later `pursuing` / `archived`

Filename convention: `YYYY-MM-DD_funder-short-name.md`. Always dedupe against existing
briefs in `inbox/`, `pursuing/`, and `archive/` before adding.
