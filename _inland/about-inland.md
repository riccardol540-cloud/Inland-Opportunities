# About INLAND

*Read this first. Everything the Scout and Producer do should be consistent with what
is written here.*

## What INLAND is

INLAND is a yearly print publication that documents the soul of a country before it
becomes history — "the landmarks, the headlines, the official story — none of that
lives here." Each issue is the result of a year-long, place-based immersion in a single
territory, told through named, locatable custodians of living traditions: artisans,
pastoralists, cooks, singers, shepherds, border families — people whose stories have
not yet been narrated internationally.

Founded in 2021. Output is a printed magazine (~170–240 pages) plus digital stories,
photography, and films. Each issue retails at €30.

Contact: info@inland-mag.com · Instagram @inland.mag · inland-mag.com

## Tagline and voice

- Public tagline: **"Travel deeper."**
- Self-description: *"Inland is a yearly publication that documents the soul of a
  country before it becomes history… We go elsewhere: into the routines, the
  contradictions, the details that don't photograph well but define everything. The
  quiet, the particular, the hard to translate. A testament of a place. Before it
  changes."*
- Voice: slow, intimate, anthropological, literary, cinematic. Restrained — never
  romanticising, never reducing a place to conflict or postcard. Foregrounds dignity
  and cultural continuity over crisis. "To understand a place is to listen to its
  people."

## Editorial method (the year-long process)

Six phases: **Grounding → Exploration → Ideation → Composition → Production →
Distribution.** In practice:

1. Months of pre-production desk research to map a territory's living traditions.
2. Identify specific, named custodians and communities tied to real places.
3. Commission photographers, writers and filmmakers to embed with them over extended
   field periods.
4. Compose into long-form visual storytelling grounded in named people and real places.

The editorial spine of every issue is **intimacy with custodians whose lives have not
been told internationally**, and a focus on endangered knowledge systems, cross-border
cultural continuity, and under-documented living practices surviving despite climate
pressure, policy change, and generational drift.

## Recurring editorial themes (use to judge fit)

- Memory, survival, and what persists despite standardization / modernization.
- Living traditions and endangered knowledge held by named individuals.
- Borderlands, minorities, displacement, return.
- Food traditions, agrarian and pastoral life, craft.
- Ritual, faith, music, and oral culture.
- Human–nature relationships and ecological pressure on culture.

## Funding model

INLAND is funded through **cultural institutions and partnerships**, not advertising.
Known and target channels include cultural institutes and foundations (e.g. Goethe
Institut, Fondazione San Lorenzo), national/EU public culture funds, and embassy /
diplomatic collaborations (the Kashmir pitch deck was built specifically for embassy
collaborators). Revenue also comes from print sales (€30/issue) and stockists.

This funding model is exactly why the upstream Scout matters: each new edition is
typically unlocked by securing an institutional partner, a public grant, or a
co-production agreement tied to a specific territory.
