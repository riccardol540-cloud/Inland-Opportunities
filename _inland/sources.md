# Sources — where the Scout looks

*A seed list of where to scan for opportunities, organised by the three agreed
channels. This is a living document: when a scan finds a productive new source, add it
here; when one proves consistently irrelevant, note it under "Low-yield".*

## Channel 1 — Public / EU culture funds

- **Creative Europe** (CULTURE strand: cooperation projects, literary translation,
  European platforms) — culture.ec.europa.eu and the EU Funding & Tenders portal.
- **Italian national funds** — Ministero della Cultura (MiC) bandi; SIAE "Per Chi
  Crea"; regional culture bandi (Lombardia and others relevant to INLAND's base).
- **Other national arts/culture councils** relevant to a target territory.
- **Private cultural foundations** — e.g. Fondazione San Lorenzo (prior funder), other
  Italian/European foundations funding photography, publishing, and heritage.
- **Photography / documentary grants** — major documentary photography grants and
  funds, magazine/independent-publishing grants (in scope when editorially aligned).

## Channel 2 — Cultural institutes

- **Goethe Institut** (prior funder) — grants, residencies, and partnership calls.
- **Institut français**, **British Council**, **Pro Helvetia**, **Camões**, and
  comparable national institutes — especially those tied to a specific target country.
- **Foundations and museums** running documentation, heritage, or photography
  programmes.

## Channel 3 — Partnerships / co-productions

- **Embassies and cultural attachés** of target countries (the Kashmir pitch was built
  for embassy collaborators — replicate that channel for other territories).
- **NGOs and heritage organisations** working on intangible cultural heritage,
  minorities, or endangered crafts in a target territory.
- **Universities and research centres** (anthropology, ethnomusicology, area studies)
  open to collaboration or co-publication.
- **Festivals and biennials** (photography, documentary, book/print) offering
  commissions or co-productions.
- **UNESCO-adjacent** intangible cultural heritage programmes and partners.

## How to scan

- Prefer official funder/institute pages and reputable open-call aggregators over
  secondary blogs. Verify the deadline and eligibility on the primary source before
  writing a brief.
- Note the access method per source (open web search, specific portal). If a relevant
  funder has a connector/MCP available, prefer it.
