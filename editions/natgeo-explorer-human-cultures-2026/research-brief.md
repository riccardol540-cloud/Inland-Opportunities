# Research brief — National Geographic Society, Explorer Grants (Human Histories & Cultures)

- **Title**: National Geographic Society — Explorer Grants (Human Histories & Cultures / storytelling)
- **Funder / host**: National Geographic Society (NGS)
- **Channel**: Public fund (grant)
- **Link**: https://www.nationalgeographic.org/funding-opportunities/grants/
- **Deadline**: Rolling with cyclical review deadlines (typically a few cycles per year). The date carried on the card (~mid-Oct 2026) is an **indicative next checkpoint only** — confirm the live Level I / Level II cycle on the NGS funding-opportunities page before treating as fixed. Not URGENT.
- **Budget / award**: Level I up to **US$20,000**; Level II up to **US$100,000**. Awarded funds may cover field expenses, equipment, and stipends for freelancers.
- **Eligibility (why INLAND qualifies)**: Open worldwide to individuals 18+ with a demonstrated record of successful media projects (portfolio required). When a project is outside the applicant's home country/community, **at least one local collaborator** must be significantly involved — a structure INLAND already uses (it commissions and partners locally). INLAND applies **via / with its commissioned edition photographer or writer**, who joins the Explorer community as the named applicant.

## Fit — 4 (Strong)

The **Human Histories & Cultures** focus area exists "to preserve cultural knowledge and better understand human history, culture, diversity… past and present" across cultural anthropology, human geography, migration and related fields, using **photography, filmmaking and journalism**. That is INLAND's exact register — place-based documentation of living traditions and cultural continuity — at production scale (up to $100k covers field embedding). Held below 5 only because the applicant must be the individual Explorer (not the magazine) and NGS storytelling grants privilege a science/exploration framing that INLAND must translate its editorial into.

## Possible territory / angle

**Open** — pairs with any in-progress or research INLAND edition. Lead candidate: **Uzbekistan** (an INLAND research edition), where the "what survived?" question has named custodians, locatable places and seasonal scenes:

- **Boysun (Surxondaryo)** — spring calendar rituals of the Boysun district, proclaimed a UNESCO Masterpiece of Intangible Cultural Heritage; a knowable seasonal scene (the Boysun Bahori festival window).
- **Bukhara** — *suzani* embroidery workshops and the last Bukharan-Jewish artisan families; a locatable craft with named masters.
- **Karakalpakstan** — life on the dried bed of the Aral Sea: fishing memory, felt and yurt culture under ecological pressure (human–nature theme).

Each thread can be framed as continuity over crisis, with the local collaborator drawn from a Tashkent/Bukhara cultural partner.
