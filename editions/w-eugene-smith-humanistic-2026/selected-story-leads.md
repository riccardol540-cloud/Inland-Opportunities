# INLAND Georgia — Selected Story Leads

*Twelve leads selected from sixteen researched, for the proposed W. Eugene Smith project
"The Quiet Country." Each is written as **Place · Angle · Practices · Status**, with named
custodians, locatable places, seasonal scenes and sources preserved. Categories follow the
INLAND house set (Arts & Culture, Food, Myth & Religion, Local communities, History,
Nature, Geopolitics).*

---

## 01 · The Road in May — *Nature*
**Place:** Kakheti / Shida Kartli winter pastures → Abano Pass (2,850 m) → Tusheti (Omalo).
**Angle:** A half-millennium-old transhumance still climbs and descends a real pass on a
real date — the project's signature recurring image, a movement of animals and people that
predates the nation.
**Practices:** The spring drive up and the autumn descent; the night camps; the dog work;
the rare *mounted* migration on horseback; alpine grazing June–September.
**Custodians:** Giorgi Karsamauli (shepherd, documented by NatGeo, 2019); the woman keeping
the horse-migration alive (RFE/RL) — re-contact and confirm 2026 ride.
**Status:** Strong; seasonal (late May / September). Confirm dates and consent.
*Sources: Atlas Obscura; National Geographic; RFE/RL; Milk Trekker.*

## 02 · The Tower and the Byre — *History*
**Place:** Ushguli (Chazhashi), Upper Svaneti — UNESCO WHC 709.
**Angle:** Families who still keep house, byre and a medieval defence tower together among
200 standing *koshki*, in one of Europe's highest inhabited places.
**Practices:** Winter stock-keeping below the tower; horse transport across roadless
country; the tower as home/fortress/treasury.
**Custodians:** Roland & Gulo Chelidze (Ushguli) — re-contact and confirm.
**Status:** Strong; accessible late spring–autumn (winter access weather-dependent).
*Sources: UNESCO WHC 709; National Geographic; Atlas Obscura; Museum of Wander.*

## 03 · Lile, the Sun Song — *Arts & Culture*
**Place:** Mestia, Svaneti.
**Angle:** Pre-Christian sun-hymns still sung — *Lile* (the Svan Sun God) and *Kviria* —
in some of the oldest layers of Georgian polyphony.
**Practices:** Choir rehearsal; three braided male voices; animist texts re-read through a
Christian lens.
**Custodians:** Riho Ensemble, dir. Vakhtang Pilpani (son of the late Islam Pilpani,
"Benefactor of Georgian Folk Song").
**Status:** Strong; rehearsals year-round. Consent to photograph.
*Sources: Georgian Chant (Kviria; Svan chant); Chanting.ge; Discogs (Riho).*

## 04 · The Highest Voice — *Arts & Culture*
**Place:** Lanchkhuti, Guria (Black Sea Georgia).
**Angle:** The *krimanchuli* yodel-falsetto — the most acrobatic voice in Georgian song —
carried as a household inheritance.
**Practices:** A *supra* table where a three-part song breaks into the krimanchuli; teaching
the high voice to the next generation.
**Custodians:** The Turkia family of Lanchkhuti, central to the Gurian repertoire.
**Status:** Strong; year-round; tie to a real feast. UNESCO ICH (2001/2008).
*Sources: UNESCO ICH 00008; chortownia.org; harmonychroniclesmag.com.*

## 05 · The Fire in the Enamel — *Arts & Culture*
**Place:** Tbilisi (the "Ornament" gallery and allied studios).
**Angle:** *Minankari* cloisonné — a medieval church technique, abandoned for centuries and
now revived by an almost unchanged hand process.
**Practices:** Laying gold wire into cells, filling and firing colour, the bench work of a
master and apprentice.
**Custodians:** Thea Gurgenidze and the "Ornament" gallery (founded 2001).
**Status:** Strong; year-round.
*Sources: NTD; mygeotrip (Minankari; Ornament); georgia.to.*

## 06 · The Wine Under the Floor — *Food*
**Place:** Kakheti — a family *marani*; the qvevri-makers of Vardisubani.
**Angle:** 8,000 years of winemaking lived, not marketed — wine fermented on its skins in
clay buried under the house, the knowledge passing through families at harvest.
**Practices:** *Rtveli* (harvest, Sept–Oct); pressing, the qvevri sealed underground; the
potter firing a vessel taller than a man.
**Custodians:** A named Kakhetian winemaking family (to confirm on the ground); Vardisubani
qvevri potters.
**Status:** Strong; seasonal (rtveli, late September). UNESCO ICH (2013), PGI (2021).
*Sources: UNESCO ICH 00870; wine.gov.ge; Decanter; winesgeorgia.com.*

## 07 · The Cheese in the Skin — *Food*
**Place:** Tusheti summer dairies (Alaznistavi cooperative).
**Angle:** A cheese born in a sheepskin on the summer mountain, and the women who run the
wool and the dairy.
**Practices:** Milking into the *guda* skin; curing under the *nabadi* felt; pardagi felt
carpets and the burka cloak.
**Custodians:** The Alaznistavi guda cooperative (EU-supported), Tusheti — name a maker.
**Status:** Strong; seasonal (June–September). Pairs with lead 01.
*Sources: EU4Georgia (Alaznistavi); Milk Trekker; Wikipedia (Tushetians).*

## 08 · The Mountain's Own Faith — *Myth & Religion*
**Place:** Khevsureti — Shatili / Mutso; the *khati* shrines of Pirikita Khevsureti.
**Angle:** A pre-Christian shrine cult surviving behind Christian names — elected priests,
horned stone sanctuaries, summer beer and offering.
**Practices:** The *khevisberi* at the horned shrine; the *dasturi* preparing the feast;
the *khatis diasakhlisi* (hostess of the shrine); **Atengenoba** ~100 days after Easter.
**Custodians:** A named khevisberi and a khatis diasakhlisi (re-contact via local networks).
**Status:** Strong; seasonal (Atengenoba ~mid-July 2026). Consent-led; ritual not spectacle.
*Sources: Wikipedia (Khevsureti); academia.edu (Khatis Diasakhlisi); khconsult.*

## 09 · The Women Who Still Sing the Zikr — *Myth & Religion*
**Place:** Duisi Old Mosque, Pankisi Gorge.
**Angle:** The only place in the South Caucasus where women lead the Sufi *zikr* — a
pacifist Chechen-Sufi rite calling "peace in the Caucasus," fading as elders pass.
**Practices:** The Friday women's circle; *nazms* in the Kist dialect; the turning chant.
**Custodians:** The Duisi women's *zikr* circle; the all-female Aznash Laaman ensemble.
**Status:** Strong but endangered; weekly (Fridays). Consent-sensitive; protect identities.
*Sources: OC Media; Al Jazeera America; pankisivalley.com.*

## 10 · The Saint in the Desert — *Geopolitics*
**Place:** David Gareja (Udabno / Lavra), Kakheti semi-desert on the Azerbaijan line.
**Angle:** A 6th-century cave monastery whose Soviet-drawn border runs straight through it —
faith, frescoes and a quiet frontier dispute in one frame. *Tension, not attack.*
**Practices:** Monastic life in the rock cells; the Queen Tamar fresco at Udabno; the ridge
where the boundary meets the painted caves.
**Custodians:** A David Gareja monk (re-contact; confirm access).
**Status:** Conditional; verify 2026 border access before scheduling Udabno.
*Sources: UNESCO tentative 5224; Advantour; Atlas Obscura; wander-lush.org.*

## 11 · The Granary Language — *Local communities*
**Place:** Mestia / Ushguli, Svaneti.
**Angle:** An endangered, unwritten mountain language thinning at home even as its songs
return to young mouths — the paradox of a youth-led revival.
**Practices:** A rehearsal where teenagers sing hymns in a tongue fewer of them now speak;
the old words kept like seed-grain.
**Custodians:** The Lileo song-and-dance ensemble and young Mestia singers.
**Status:** Strong; year-round. Distinct from lead 03 (this is language & generation).
*Sources: Chai Khana; georgianchant.org; UNESCO Atlas of endangered languages.*

## 12 · 2,600 Years of Welcome — *Local communities*
**Place:** Oni Synagogue (Racha); Kutaisi Jewish quarter (Boris Gaponov St).
**Angle:** One of the world's oldest diasporas — in the country that says it never
persecuted them — thinning toward a last minyan.
**Practices:** A Sabbath where the minyan is barely made, in a synagogue built for a
thousand; the keepers of the 19th-century synagogues.
**Custodians:** The Oni and Kutaisi Jewish communities (re-contact via community leaders).
**Status:** Strong but endangered; year-round (confirm festival dates). Discretion.
*Sources: Wikipedia (Oni; Kutaisi synagogues); Gil Travel; jguideeurope.org.*
