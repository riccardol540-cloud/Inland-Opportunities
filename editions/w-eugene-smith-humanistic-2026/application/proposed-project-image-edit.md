# Application — Proposed-project image edit
### W. Eugene Smith Grant 2026 · "The Quiet Country"

> The Smith form takes **up to 40 images total** (past work + proposed project combined).
> This is the **shot edit for the proposed project** — a frame-by-frame target the
> photographer shoots toward in the field, and from which the final submission run is cut.
> These are *intended* frames, grounded in the research; captions are written to be
> completed on the ground once the named protagonist consents. Aim to deliver **~22–24** of
> these to sit alongside the ~16–18 past-work frames (confirm the 2026 split).

Order follows the essay's journey (the three clusters), opening and closing on the
signature image. Each entry: **frame — intended content — note.**

### Editing principles (for the cut to ~22–24)
- Keep **one strong run** on a single protagonist (likely the shepherd family) so the edit
  shows depth, not breadth alone — the quality the jury rewards.
- Balance **portrait / scene / detail / landscape**; avoid two frames doing the same job.
- Privilege **the undramatised decisive moment** over spectacle; light and gesture carry it.
- Every published frame: **named, consented, captioned** (place · who · what · season/year).
