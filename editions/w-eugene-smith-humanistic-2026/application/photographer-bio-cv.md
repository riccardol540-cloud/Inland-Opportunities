# Application — Photographer bio & CV
### W. Eugene Smith Grant 2026

> The Smith form asks for a **biography (≤1,500 characters)** and a **CV (≤2,000
> characters, or a PDF of any length)**. The applicant is an **individual photographer** —
> the INLAND-commissioned photographer on the Georgia work. Because the named applicant is
> still to be confirmed by INLAND, the two blocks below are **fill-in templates** written to
> the character limits, with guidance. Replace every `[…]` and re-check the counts before
> submitting.

---

## Biography  *(field limit: 1,500 characters)*

> Write in the third person, restrained and factual, in INLAND's voice. Foreground the
> *sustained, humanistic* nature of the work; name the Georgia body of work; end on what
> drives the practice. Template (≈ fill to ~1,200–1,400 characters):

`[NAME]` is a documentary photographer based in `[CITY, COUNTRY]`, working on long-form,
place-based essays about people who keep living traditions alive. `[Their]` work follows a
small number of named custodians over extended field periods, in a slow, intimate register
concerned with the dignity of ordinary life.

`[NAME]` photographed `[INLAND's Georgia edition (Issue 02, 2024) / a defined body of the
Georgia work]`, embedding with `[shepherds / tower families / singers — name the communities]`
across the country's highlands. `[Earlier projects: 1–2 lines, each a cohesive body of work
with place + subject + where shown/published.]`

`[Their]` photographs have been `[published in / exhibited at — list 2–3 venues or titles]`.
`[NAME]` works primarily in `[stills / stills and film; B&W / colour]`, returning to subjects
across seasons so the camera is present for the real moment. The proposed project, "The
Quiet Country," continues this commitment in Georgia — concerned photography of the human
condition, made with custodians rather than about them.
