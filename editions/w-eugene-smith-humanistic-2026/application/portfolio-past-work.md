# Application — Portfolio of past work
### W. Eugene Smith Grant 2026 · the prior cohesive project

> The Smith Grant asks for a **portfolio of past work** demonstrating a prior cohesive
> project in the humanistic tradition. INLAND's route is to submit the applicant
> photographer's body of work from **INLAND Issue 02 — Georgia (2024)**, the published
> edition this proposal extends. This document specifies the edit to assemble; the actual
> frames are the photographer's own from that edition (to be selected with the editor).

---

## The prior cohesive project: INLAND Issue 02 — Georgia (2024)

- **Format:** printed edition, 196 pages, 8 stories, 14 contributors; retails at €30.
- **Register:** slow, place-based, custodian-led documentary — the same humanistic essay
  mode the Smith Fund honours.
- **Why it qualifies as a cohesive project:** a single territory, photographed over an
  extended field period, organised around named people and real places rather than topics —
  a finished, published long-form body of work, not a sampler.

## How to build the portfolio edit (the submission)

The Smith form allows the past-work and proposed-project images to total **up to 40
frames**. Suggested split: **~16–18 frames of past work** (Issue 02 Georgia) + **~22–24
frames of the proposed project** (see `proposed-project-image-edit.md`). Confirm the exact
split the 2026 form allows before finalising.

Select the past-work edit to evidence the qualities the jury reads for:

1. **Intimacy and consent** — frames made *inside* a life (a kitchen, a byre, a rehearsal),
   not from the road. The relationship should be visible in the picture.
2. **The decisive, undramatised moment** — Smith's register: significance without spectacle;
   light and gesture doing the work.
3. **Place as character** — the Georgian highland landscape and interiors as more than
   backdrop; weather, altitude, season legible in the frame.
4. **A through-line** — an edit that reads as one author's sustained vision across the issue,
   so it sits naturally beside the proposed Georgia continuation.

## Sequencing note

Open with one or two of the strongest single images from Issue 02, then a short, coherent
run that shows depth on **one** custodian or community (a mini-essay within the portfolio) —
juries reward evidence that the photographer can stay with a subject. Avoid a "greatest
hits" spread of unrelated single frames.

## Captions

Each image: **place, who is pictured (with consent), what is happening, season/year.** Keep
captions factual and restrained, in INLAND's voice — name people and places; no adjectives
doing the picture's job.
