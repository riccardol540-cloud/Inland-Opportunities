# Research brief — Strategia Fotografia (MiC / DGCC) — recurring national fund [RADAR]

- **Title:** Strategia Fotografia — selection of proposals for acquisition, commissioning and
  promotion of photography
- **Funder / host:** Ministero della Cultura — Direzione Generale Creatività Contemporanea (DGCC)
- **Channel:** Public fund (Italian national)
- **Link:** https://creativitacontemporanea.cultura.gov.it/strategia-fotografia/
- **Status of the current cycle:** The **2026 (6th) edition closed 15 April 2026** — that window
  is passed. This is an **annual, recurring** call; the deadline below is an **estimate for the
  next (2027) edition**, which in recent years has opened in late February and closed in mid-April.
  **Confirm exact 2027 dates when the DGCC posts the new avviso.**
- **Estimated next deadline:** ~15 April 2027 (provisional — recurring annual call)
- **Budget (2026 reference):** **€2,000,000 total** — €1,300,000 for Acquisition & Commissioning,
  €700,000 for Promotion.

## Structure (2026 reference edition)

**Area 1 — Acquisition & Commissioning (€1.3M)**
- *Section I* — contributions to acquire photographic works, projects, collections, archives and
  funds (contemporary and historical), to be assigned to an **Italian public collection**.
- *Section II* — contributions to **commission new photographic works or projects**, to be
  assigned to an Italian public collection.

**Area 2 — Promotion of photography in Italy and abroad (€700k)**
- *Section III* — contributions to organise **photographic exhibitions** in Italy and/or abroad.
- *Section IV* — contributions for **editorial productions dedicated to photography** (i.e. a
  photography publication).

## Fit for INLAND — score 4/5

Strong and strategically important — arguably the single most on-profile **Italian national fund**
for INLAND, and currently absent from the board (which otherwise leans on foreign photo prizes).
Section IV (editorial photography productions) maps directly onto funding an INLAND issue, and
Section II (commissioning new photographic work) maps onto commissioning an edition's field
photography. Held at 4, not 5, because Area 1 requires the work to be **assigned to an Italian
public collection** — a structural condition that needs the right public-institution partner — and
because the eligibility of a private cultural producer/publisher must be confirmed on the official
bando (Area 2 promotion is typically the more accessible route for an operator like INLAND).

## Candidate territory / INLAND angle

**An Italian (or Italy-anchored) edition**, since the fund promotes Italian photography and Area 1
outputs go to Italian public collections. Building on Issue 03 (*Isole Minori*), candidates:

- **The Arbëreshë** of Calabria / Basilicata / Sicily — endangered Albanian-speaking, Byzantine-rite
  communities; ritual laments, Easter *Vallja* dances (also see the Allianz entry).
- **Sardinian pastoral & lagoon cultures** — transhumance, *fassoni* reed-boat fishers, *bottarga*.
- **The Appennine *aree interne*** — depopulating inland Italy, transhumance routes, agrarian ritual.

Route: commission new photographic work (Section II) destined for an Italian public collection,
paired with an editorial photography production (Section IV) — i.e. the INLAND issue itself.
