# Research brief — Tom Stoddart Award for Excellence (Ian Parry Fund × GOST Books)

- **Title:** The Tom Stoddart Award for Excellence, in collaboration with GOST Books
- **Funder / host:** The Ian Parry Photojournalism Fund + GOST Books (publisher); media partner **The Guardian**
- **Channel:** public-fund (documentary photobook prize)
- **Link:** https://www.ianparry.org/the-tom-stoddart-award-in-collaboration-with-gost-books · apply: https://site.picter.com/ippg-tomstoddart-gost-photobook-grant-2026
- **Deadline:** 2026-09-30 (23:59) — *open now*
- **Award:** A photo book published by **GOST Books**, **£5,000** toward completing the project, an expenses-paid trip to oversee printing in **Verona**, and work published by **The Guardian**.

## Eligibility (why INLAND qualifies)
Open worldwide, 18+, to **established photographers working in long-form documentary photography** with an in-depth project *nearing completion* and extensive enough to warrant a book. INLAND applies **via its commissioned edition photographer**, exactly as with the W. Eugene Smith / Lucie / PhMuseum routes — the applicant is the photographer, carrying an edition's body of work.

## Fit — 4/5
INLAND is, at bottom, a long-form documentary **photobook** publisher; a "project nearing completion, warranting a book" is the literal description of an INLAND edition's photographic spine. GOST is a serious art-book house and *The Guardian* publication is real international visibility. Not edition *production* money (it rewards near-finished work), and the applicant must be the photographer — so it works best paired with an edition whose field shoot is largely done (e.g. the Georgia body of work behind the Smith entry, or Issue 03 Isole Minori). Held below 5 only because it funds completion/printing rather than the field year.

## Possible territory / angle
Open — pair with whichever in-progress edition has the most complete photographic body of work. Lead candidates: **Georgia** (Issue 02 + the highland custodians researched for the Smith grant) or **Isole Minori** (Issue 03), reframed as a single cohesive book-length documentary essay on one custodian community.

## Required application documents
- Short photographer biography
- Book project description (the edition's body of work as a book)
- Up to **40 images** (the proposed book edit)
