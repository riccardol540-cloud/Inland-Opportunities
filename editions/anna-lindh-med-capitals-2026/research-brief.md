# Research brief — Anna Lindh Foundation · Mediterranean Capitals of Culture & Dialogue 2026 (2nd Call)

- **Title**: Mediterranean Capitals of Culture & Dialogue 2026 — 2nd Call (Matera & Tétouan)
- **Funder / host**: Anna Lindh Euro-Mediterranean Foundation for the Dialogue between Cultures (ALF)
- **Channel**: partnership
- **Link**: https://on-the-move.org/news/anna-lindh-foundation-projects-mediterranean-capitals-culture-2026-italy-morocco
- **Deadline**: **2026-07-04** — ⚠️ **URGENT** (≤ 21 days from scan)
- **Budget / award size**: Small scale up to **€2,500** (max 3 people); Medium scale **€2,500–5,000** (4–5 people). Small.
- **Eligibility (why INLAND qualifies)**: Intercultural entities (or equivalent) from the 43 Euro-Mediterranean countries able to design and run cultural/intercultural activities in **Matera (Italy)** or **Tétouan (Morocco)**. Activities must be in indoor venues or outdoor public space, **non-profit, no ticket sales**. As an Italy-based cultural producer, INLAND is eligible.
- **Fit score**: **3 — Possible**
- **Rationale**: Both cities are genuine INLAND territory (Matera/Basilicata's grain, ritual and transhumance cultures; Tétouan as the gateway to the Rif and Jbala/Andalusian heritage). **But** the call funds *participatory intercultural activities/dialogue events in the city*, not a publication, and the budget is small — best read as a **pilot / relationship foothold and field-scouting subsidy**, not edition funding.

## Possible territory / angle

- **Tétouan & the Rif (Morocco)** — strongest INLAND angle: a small participatory documentation/listening event with Jbala custodians (weaving, mountain song, agrarian ritual), doubling as scouting for a fuller Mediterranean edition (pairs naturally with the Institut français Mediterranean photo aid on the board).
- **Matera (Italy)** — a public listening/exhibition activity with Basilicata custodians (grain, transhumance, sacred ritual), low-cost given INLAND's Italian base.
