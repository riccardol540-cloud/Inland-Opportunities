# Research brief — Goethe-Institut International Coproduction Fund (IKF), Round 2 (2026)

**Channel:** Cultural institute · **Funder:** Goethe-Institut (Munich, Cultural Dept.)
**Link:** https://www.goethe.de/en/kul/foe/int/ikf.html
**Round 2 window:** mid-July – 1 October 2026 (projects starting 2027, ending by 1 April 2028)
**Grant:** €15,000–30,000 (artist fees, travel, production)
**Fit:** 4/5

## Why it fits INLAND
The IKF funds artistically driven work built on *genuine cross-border collaboration* with
strong public impact — structurally the same as an INLAND edition co-produced with an
in-country partner (the model used for the Kashmir embassy pitch). Goethe-Institut is a
prior INLAND funder, so the relationship and the house language already exist.

## Eligibility / shape
Cross-border collaboration involving a German/Goethe partner; covers fees, travel and
production. INLAND would apply as the European producer paired with a partner tied to the
target territory.

## What an application needs
Project description, budget, timeline, a co-production/partner agreement, and team CVs.

## Notes for the team
- This is the production-scale Goethe instrument (vs. the smaller mobility grants).
- Strongest if paired with a concrete partner + territory before the Round 2 window opens.
