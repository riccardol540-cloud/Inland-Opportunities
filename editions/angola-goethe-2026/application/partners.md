## The partners

### European partner / applicant — INLAND
- Yearly publication documenting the soul of countries before they change (Faroe Islands 2023,
  Georgia 2024, Italy's minor islands 2025). Italy / EU-based — eligible as an EU-based cultural
  actor. **[confirm legal entity, registration, lead applicant name]**
- **Role:** editorial direction, photography/film commissioning, design, publishing, and the
  European leg of the public presentation.

### African partner — Lueji A'Nkonde University (Lunda Norte, Angola) **[confirm]**
- The Angolan public university that **coordinated the successful UNESCO Intangible Cultural
  Heritage nomination of the Chokwe *sona* sand-writing tradition (2023)** with the Ministry of
  Culture — a documented, credible custodian-facing institution rooted in the Lunda art
  heartland.
- **Role:** co-creation and co-selection, community access and consent (sona masters, Chokwe
  *sobas* and *mukanda* communities), language and cultural interpretation, and the Angolan leg
  of the public presentation.
- **Why this partner:** an established, locally-rooted institution with a proven heritage-
  safeguarding record gives the project equitable Angolan authorship and a credible in-country
  presentation route — central to the programme's co-creation principle.

## Balance of the partnership (equity statement)

Authorship, editorial selection, and dissemination are shared. The Angolan partner is a named
co-author of the edition and the exhibition, not a fixer; custodians are credited collaborators.
Decisions on which stories proceed — especially sensitive ones (the *efundula* initiation, the
*mukanda* seclusion, the Kianda rite) — rest jointly with the partner and the communities.
