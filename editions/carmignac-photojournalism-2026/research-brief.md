# Research brief — Carmignac Photojournalism Award, 15th Edition (Southeast Asia)

- **Funder / host:** Fondation Carmignac
- **Channel:** Public fund (photojournalism award / production grant)
- **Link:** https://www.fondationcarmignac.com/en/photojournalism-award/
- **Deadline:** 19 August 2026
- **Budget / award:** €50,000 production grant + a travelling exhibition and a monograph (premiered at the Visa pour l'Image festival, Perpignan)
- **Fit score:** 2 / 5 (stretch — surfaced for the humans to weigh)

## Why it's on the board
Big budget (€50,000 plus full production, book, and a major festival exhibition), open to photographers of all nationalities, individual or collaborative — so INLAND-eligible via a commissioned photographer. The 15th edition's territory is **Southeast Asia**, where coastal and fishing communities hold rich, under-documented living traditions that *could* read as INLAND.

## Why it's only a 2 (the honest caveat)
The 15th edition's brief is **investigative reporting on illegal fishing / overfishing and the related human and environmental rights violations**. That is an adversarial, exposé register — the opposite of INLAND's discipline of *continuity over crisis, dignity over spectacle, tension not attack*. Winning work must serve the set investigative theme, which would force INLAND off its editorial spine. Worth knowing about for the budget and the territory, but a genuine reframing risk.

## Candidate territory / angle
Southeast Asia (coastal / fishing communities) — fixed by the 15th-edition theme. If pursued, the only INLAND-compatible angle would centre named custodians and the living maritime culture (boatbuilding, salt, fermented-fish traditions, sea-nomad knowledge) with the rights-violation story kept as background — which may not satisfy the award's investigative brief.

## Required application documents
- Project proposal on the set theme
- Portfolio of past work
- Budget / production plan
- CV
- Letter of intent
