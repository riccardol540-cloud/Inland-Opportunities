# Research brief — Allianz Kulturstiftung: "The Art of Bringing Europe Together"

- **Title**: The Art of Bringing Europe Together — project grants for translocal European cultural cooperation
- **Funder / host**: Allianz Kulturstiftung (Allianz Foundation), Berlin
- **Channel**: public-fund (private cultural foundation)
- **Link**: https://on-the-move.org/news/allianz-kulturstiftung-call-projects-art-bringing-europe-together
- **Deadline**: Rolling — proposals accepted year-round; the board decides twice a year (announcements in **July** and **December**). Listed deadline field points at the next December 2026 decision cycle; there is no hard cut-off.
- **Budget / award size**: Requested grant **€40,000–€80,000**, covering **no more than 50%** of total project costs (co-financing required for the remainder).
- **Eligibility**: European cultural actors running **translocal** projects — partners from **different countries or regions** collaborating. The foundation funds **non-profit partners and projects**; INLAND qualifies by partnering with (or constituting the project through) a non-profit cultural body and at least one cross-border partner.

## Possible territory / angle (per research-angle.md)

A genuinely **translocal European** subject where the INLAND angle already exists — the strongest candidate is the **Arbëreshë**: the Albanian-speaking, Byzantine-rite communities of southern Italy (Calabria, Basilicata, Sicily — e.g. Civita, San Demetrio Corone, Piana degli Albanesi) in living dialogue with **Albania**. This is authentically translocal (Italy ↔ Albania), a minority holding an endangered language, and rich in photographable seasonal ritual:

- **Endangered language & oral form** — *arbërisht* spoken by named elders; the *vjershë* / *vajtim* (ritual laments) held by women singers.
- **Ritual & faith that persisted** — the Italo-Albanian Byzantine rite; Easter/*Pashkët* processions and the *Vallja* dances in costume (a fixed seasonal scene, photographable).
- **Borderland / diaspora memory** — five centuries after the 15th-century migration, a community that "stayed Albanian" inside Italy; the return-and-exchange thread with Albanian partners supplies the cross-border half the funder wants.

Field-verification needs: name the living custodians (a *cantore*, a costume-maker, a papàs), confirm the seasonal calendar, and secure a non-profit + Albanian partner to satisfy the translocal/non-profit conditions. The loud story (migration, minority politics) stays background; the lens is continuity.
