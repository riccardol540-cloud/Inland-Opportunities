# Research brief — Goethe-Institut Africa–Europe Partnerships for Culture (2026)

**Channel:** Cultural institute / partnership · **Funder:** Goethe-Institut
**Link:** https://on-the-move.org/news/goethe-institut-mobility-grants-artists-and-culture-professionals-sub-saharan-africa-and
**Programme:** €8M, 2025–2028. Two relevant strands:
- **Mobility grants** — up to €4,000 (travel/collaboration). Rolling quarterly deadlines; next **15 September 2026** (then 15 December).
- **Visual Arts grant** — larger, production-scale, for collaborative SSA–Europe visual projects.
**Fit:** 4/5 · **Territory:** Rwanda (in-progress edition)

## Why it fits INLAND
Direct territory match for the active Rwanda edition: the programme funds equitable
Africa–Europe co-creation. The mobility grant is sized for a scouting/field trip with a
Rwandan partner; the Visual Arts strand is the route to production funding.

## Eligibility / shape
Artists and cultural professionals collaborating between Sub-Saharan Africa and Europe.
INLAND (Europe-based) would partner with Rwandan creatives/custodians already mapped in
the Rwanda research.

## What an application needs
Project description, confirmation of an African + European partner, budget.

## Notes for the team
- Quick win path: use a mobility grant to fund a Rwanda scouting trip, then pursue the
  Visual Arts strand for the edition itself.
- Pair with named custodians from the existing Rwanda topic list to make it concrete.
