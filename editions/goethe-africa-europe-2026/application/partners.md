# Partnership & partner confirmation — INLAND Rwanda
### Goethe-Institut · Africa–Europe Partnerships for Culture

*The programme requires a confirmed cross-border partnership (an African + a European
partner). This file states the partnership and provides **draft confirmation-letter
templates**. The actual signed letters must be obtained before submission — see
"Action required".*

---

## The partners

### European partner / applicant — INLAND
- Yearly publication documenting the soul of countries before they change (Faroe Islands
  2023, Georgia 2024, Italy's minor islands 2025). Italy / EU-based — eligible as an
  EU-based cultural actor. **[confirm legal entity, registration, lead applicant name]**
- **Role:** editorial direction, photography/film commissioning, design, publishing, and
  the European leg of the public presentation.

### African partner — Red Rocks Initiatives / Red Rocks Cultural Centre (Musanze, Rwanda) **[confirm]**
- A Rwandan cultural organisation hosting artist residencies, community cultural
  programmes and sustainable cultural tourism, with deep links to communities around
  Musanze (Batwa, brewing and craft custodians).
- **Role:** co-creation and co-selection, community access and consent, language and
  cultural interpretation, custodian liaison, and the Rwandan leg of the public
  presentation.
- **Why this partner:** an established, locally-rooted cultural host gives the project
  equitable Rwandan authorship and a credible in-country presentation venue — central to
  the programme's co-creation principle.

**Alternate / additional partners by theme** (in case Red Rocks is unavailable or a
co-partner is wanted): **AIMPO** (Batwa stories & consent), **Slow Food Rwanda** (food
heritage), **Haguruka** (women & land). See `../INLAND_Rwanda_Topics_Contacts.xlsx`
(Contacts sheet).

## Balance of the partnership (equity statement)

Authorship, editorial selection, and dissemination are shared. The Rwandan partner is a
named co-author of the edition and the exhibition, not a fixer; custodians are credited
collaborators. Decisions on which stories proceed — especially sensitive ones — rest
jointly with the partner and the communities.
