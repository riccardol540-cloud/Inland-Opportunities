# INLAND Rwanda — Selected Story Leads

*The curated subset chosen for editorial development from the deep-research corpus. Twelve
leads, balanced across INLAND's categories. Each is structured as **Place · Angle ·
Practices · Status**, preserving the research trail. For the Goethe Africa–Europe Visual
Arts application, leads marked ★ form the proposed first production cluster.*

---

## 01 ★ The Batwa — What They Carry
**Category:** Local communities / Nature

- **Place:** Batwa settlements in **Musanze District** (short motorbike from Musanze town);
  potters documented in **Kibingo**. Access via **AIMPO** and **Red Rocks** (Musanze).
- **Angle:** Not the displacement story (already filmed as *Nyama*, 2022) but **the
  knowledge itself** — elders' plant identification, animal behaviour and spatial memory of
  the Nyungwe and Virunga forests, now sought by conservation biologists. What happens to
  forest knowledge when there is no forest left to use it in.
- **Practices:** Ethnobotany; pottery; under-recorded polyphonic forest songs (UNESCO angle);
  the tension between authentic expression and "Dancing Pots" performance staged for survival.
- **Status:** Living but precarious; ~25,000–30,000 Batwa, officially "historically
  marginalised people"; ecological-knowledge angle essentially unreported.

## 02 ★ The Rites That Won't Die — Kubandwa / Ryangombe
**Category:** Myth & Religion

- **Place:** Discreet fieldwork in and around **Kigali**; academic introductions via
  **Cecilia Pennacini** (Univ. of Turin) and **Simon Bizimana** (RCSP Rwanda).
- **Angle:** A pre-colonial spirit-possession system that missionaries tried to erase,
  surviving underground in one of Africa's most Christianised countries (92.8%). A 2021
  academic recorded students attending rites "even in the centre of Kigali."
- **Practices:** Spirit possession centred on Ryangombe; initiation; adaptive ritual that
  crosses "linguistic, political and temporal frontiers."
- **Status:** Strongly suggested living practice; **needs field confirmation**; must be
  approached with consent and discretion. If confirmed, the edition's signature story.

## 03 ★ The Hospital Will Send You to a Healer — *uburozi*
**Category:** Myth & Religion / Local communities

- **Place:** **Kinigi, Musanze** — "the core location for traditional healers"; villages
  **Burera, Bushozi, Musenyi, Rugali** (named in the 2021 study).
- **Angle:** A state-acknowledged parallel healthcare system operating in the open:
  hospitals referring patients to healers for *uburozi*, a sorcery/poisoning illness
  conventional medicine doesn't recognise.
- **Practices:** Diagnosis and treatment of *uburozi*; the healer–hospital referral; the
  2022 Traditional Medicine policy and emerging regulatory council.
- **Status:** Living and state-acknowledged (~5,000 healers nationally, per a March 2026
  parliamentary statement); almost completely unreported internationally.

## 04 ★ The Women Who Crush Bananas With Their Feet — *urwagwa*
**Category:** Food / Local communities

- **Place:** **Mushubati Sector, Rutsiro District** — named protagonist **Agatha Tuyisenge**,
  mother of five, who built a livelihood from inherited banana-beer knowledge (documented by
  SENS Magazine).
- **Angle:** Banana beer not as a drink but as ritual infrastructure — the medium of
  marriage negotiations, ancestral offerings, reconciliation. Follow one full brewing cycle
  toward a wedding.
- **Practices:** Selecting banana varieties (Kamala, Fiya, Ibitokatoki); foot-crushing;
  sorghum fermentation; the ceremonial economy around the beer.
- **Status:** Living but displaced by commercial beer (Heineken, Skol) and crop
  standardisation squeezing beer-banana groves.

## 05 The Last Sorghum Beer on the Volcano Slopes — *ikigage*
**Category:** Food / Local communities

- **Place:** Households near **Kinigi and Musanze**; brewing visits via **Amahoro Tours /
  Red Rocks**.
- **Angle:** A sorghum beer of feasts, dowries and harvests, now on the **Slow Food Ark of
  Taste**. A household that still grows sorghum and brews ikigage for weddings is a
  compressed portrait of what agricultural policy does to cultural memory.
- **Practices:** Sorghum cultivation and malting; communal brewing; feast and dowry use.
- **Status:** Endangered; declining with sorghum cultivation under the Crop Intensification
  Programme.

## 06 The Sacred Harvest Stripped of Its Secrets — *Umuganura*
**Category:** History / Myth & Religion

- **Place:** The ritual site **Huro, Bumbogo (now Gakenke District)**; national celebration
  the **first Friday of August**.
- **Angle:** A sacred rite of *ubwiru* guarded by the hereditary **Abiru**, abolished by the
  Belgians in 1925 (last ritualist **Gashamura** exiled), revived in 2011 as a secular
  holiday — without the Abiru, the sacred sorghum, or the secret language. A ceremony
  hollowed of meaning and repurposed for nation-building.
- **Practices:** The historic rite of cosmic renewal; the contemporary state festival; the
  intact-but-vacant physical site.
- **Status:** The folk-national version is alive; the sacred core is broken — a story about
  what revival leaves out.

## 07 The Morality of Milk — *kivuguto*
**Category:** Food / Local communities

- **Place:** Pastoral households (rural; pairs naturally with the Musanze or Gishwati base).
- **Angle:** Fermented milk in handmade **inkongoro** gourds carrying unique indigenous
  microbial cultures, against footage inside a modern dairy plant making packaged "yogurt
  drinks." The moral order encoded in how milk is stored, fermented and shared.
- **Practices:** Morning milking; gourd fermentation; hospitality, blessing and initiation
  uses.
- **Status:** Living but industrialising; gourds and indigenous cultures replaced by steel
  and controlled starters.

## 08 ★ Life After the Forest — the Gishwati resettlement
**Category:** Local communities / Nature

- **Place:** Resettlement sites in **Nyabihu, Rutsiro, Rubavu**; Nyabihu sectors **Bigogwe,
  Jenda, Rambura**; named interlocutor **Laurent Hategekimana** (Nyabihu).
- **Angle:** Families moved off the Gishwati–Mukura forests now farm ~0.5 ha on steep,
  flood-prone slopes; some patrol as unpaid conservation auxiliaries the forest margins
  their families were removed from. The long, quiet afterlife of displacement.
- **Practices:** Smallholder farming on piedmont slopes; volunteer forest patrol;
  resettlement-village life.
- **Status:** Living and precarious; a landscape remade repeatedly by ranching, encroachment,
  floods and now a flagship restoration park.

## 09 Thirty Years On — the reconciliation coffee cooperatives
**Category:** Food / History / Local communities

- **Place:** **Maraba, Huye** (Cyarumbo, Sovu, Kibingo stations) and **Dukunde Kawa Musasa,
  Gakenke** (Musasa, Ruli, Mbilima stations; women-grown-coffee groups).
- **Angle:** Cooperatives built post-1994 to put Hutu and Tutsi farmers in the same washing
  stations — origin story told, present unfilmed. Founders now middle-aged; their children
  work side by side. What changed, what didn't.
- **Practices:** Hand-picking, sorting and washing on volcanic hillsides; cooperative
  governance; quality grading and premiums funding schooling and health cover.
- **Status:** Thriving institutionally; the human texture under-documented. Harvest season
  for the visual core.

## 10 The Last Maestro — *inanga*
**Category:** Arts & Culture

- **Place:** **Gatagara** — **Vianey Mushabizi**, possibly the last living master of the
  inanga zither.
- **Angle:** A single instrument and the man who may be the end of its line; the sound of a
  pre-radio Rwanda held in one pair of hands.
- **Practices:** Inanga playing, tuning and repertoire; oral transmission (or its absence).
- **Status:** Critically endangered; depends on one or two named practitioners.

## 11 Geometry of the Widows — *imigongo*
**Category:** Arts & Culture / History

- **Place:** **Kakira, Eastern Province** — a cooperative of genocide widows.
- **Angle:** Geometric cow-dung paintings made by widows, now commercialised; the tension
  between cultural preservation, livelihood and market co-option.
- **Practices:** Cow-dung relief painting; cooperative production; the craft's domestic and
  ceremonial origins.
- **Status:** Living and commercialising — a story about who profits when a tradition scales.

## 12 The Data Centre and the Hill
**Category:** Geopolitics / Local communities

- **Place:** An industrial/landscape site near recently electrified villages (Eastern
  Province / peri-Kigali).
- **Angle:** A windowless server warehouse running 24h at full load, a few kilometres from
  villages where electricity arrived three years ago; the guard at the gate is from such a
  village. Two relationships to electricity, land and the future, coexisting.
- **Practices:** Everyday rural electrification; the invisible labour around data
  infrastructure.
- **Status:** Living present; needs a human anchor to avoid abstraction.
