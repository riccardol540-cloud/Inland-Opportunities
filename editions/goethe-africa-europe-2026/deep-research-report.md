# INLAND Rwanda — Deep-Research Report

*Prepared for the Goethe-Institut **Africa–Europe Partnerships for Culture** application
(Visual Arts / Mobility strands). Produced by the INLAND Producer, 2026-06-22, drawing on
the existing INLAND Rwanda research corpus (`../Rwanda/`) and the sources cited throughout.*

---

## TL;DR

Rwanda is internationally legible through two stories — the 1994 genocide and the
"African Singapore" development miracle. Both are loud; both crowd out the quieter
country. INLAND's Rwanda edition asks a single question across every lead:
**what survived?** What persists in specific families, specific villages, and specific
practices that has been absorbed by neither the colonial inheritance, the Christian
mission, nor the post-1994 modernisation project — and can still be *witnessed and
photographed* today.

This is not a critique of the state. It is an act of listening — and it is precisely the
kind of equitable, co-created Africa–Europe cultural work the Goethe programme exists to
fund: a Rwandan cultural partner and a European publication building a long-form visual
record of living Rwandan traditions, made *with* custodians rather than *about* them.

The corpus below yields **20 researched leads**, of which **12 are selected** for the
edition (see `selected-story-leads.md`). Each one has a **named protagonist or custodian
community, a locatable place, and a visual scene that exists in a real season.**

---

## Editorial frame

> **Memory, survival, and what persists despite standardisation.**

Rwanda has passed through successive ruptures — Belgian colonialism, Christian missions,
the 1994 genocide, and a deliberate post-genocide project that built a single unified
"Rwandan" identity over older ethnic and traditional ones. Each wave standardised, erased,
or replaced what came before. The edition documents what is still here.

**Constraints carried from the research sessions (honour these in field and pitch):**
- **The genocide is context, never the lens.** International coverage is saturated; it may
  appear as background but is never the primary subject of a story.
- **No adversarial framing of the state.** A tourism-ministry partnership is in discussion;
  stories can be complex without casting the government as villain. Tension, not attack.
- **Quality bar:** every topic needs a specific protagonist, a locatable place, and a
  visual scene. No abstract narratives.
- **Geography is open** — the whole country is in scope, with natural clusters around
  Musanze (north), Huye/Gakenke (coffee south), and Kigali.

## Key findings

1. **Batwa ecological knowledge is the strongest under-told story in the country.** The
   displacement narrative is already filmed (*Nyama*, dir. Rosen & Mugisha, 2022). The
   fresh territory is **the knowledge itself** — Batwa elders around **Musanze** hold
   centuries-deep plant identification, animal behaviour and spatial memory of the Nyungwe
   and Virunga forests that exists nowhere else, now being sought by conservation
   biologists before it disappears. Estimated 25,000–30,000 Batwa; advocacy via **AIMPO**;
   documented potters in **Kibingo**; under-recorded polyphonic forest songs (a UNESCO
   angle).

2. **Pre-colonial religion did not die — it went underground.** *Kubandwa*, the
   spirit-possession system centred on **Ryangombe**, was systematically targeted by
   missionaries; Rwanda is now 92.8% Christian. Yet a Rwandan academic recorded (2021)
   students attending Kubandwa rites "even in the centre of Kigali." Scholarship
   (Cecilia Pennacini, Univ. of Turin; Simon Bizimana, RCSP Rwanda 2018) confirms the
   tradition is "inherently adaptive." No journalist has investigated this. **Needs
   fieldwork to confirm; if confirmed, it is extraordinary.**

3. **A state-acknowledged parallel healthcare system is operating in the open and
   unreported.** A 2021 peer-reviewed study (*BMC Complementary Medicine and Therapies*)
   documented hospitals in **Kinigi, Musanze** referring patients to traditional healers
   for *uburozi*, a culture-specific poisoning/sorcery illness. In March 2026 Rwanda's
   Health Minister told parliament ~5,000 healers operate nationally; a Traditional
   Medicine policy (2022) and regulatory council exist. Kinigi is "the core location for
   traditional healers in the country" — a very locatable entry point.

4. **Food is the densest reservoir of ritual memory** — and the most visual. Four linked
   leads: **urwagwa** (banana beer crushed by foot — named brewer **Agatha Tuyisenge**,
   Mushubati, Rutsiro); **ikigage** (sorghum beer near Kinigi/Musanze, on the Slow Food
   Ark of Taste); **kivuguto** (fermented milk in handmade *inkongoro* gourds carrying
   indigenous microbial cultures); and **near-extinct grains** grown by farmer
   **Bernard Bizimana** in Rulindo. Each is being squeezed out by the Crop Intensification
   Programme and commercial substitutes.

5. **The state has repurposed sacred ritual as nation-building, leaving the physical sites
   intact but hollow.** *Umuganura*, the harvest festival, was one of the 18 sacred rites
   of *ubwiru* guarded by the hereditary **Abiru**, requiring sacred sorghum from **Huro**
   in Bumbogo (now Gakenke). Abolished by the Belgians in 1925 (last ritualist,
   **Gashamura**, exiled); revived in 2011 as a secular holiday — without the Abiru, the
   plants, or the secret language. **Huro still exists, present but spiritually vacant.**

6. **Rwanda's development showcase has a photographable underside.** Data centres, the
   Carnegie Mellon Africa campus, Zipline drone deliveries — too abstract for INLAND alone,
   but powerful anchored in one human frame: a windowless server warehouse running 24h at
   full load a few kilometres from villages electrified only three years ago. The guard at
   the gate is from such a village. **Two relationships to electricity, land and the future,
   coexisting.**

7. **Displacement has a long, quiet afterlife in the landscape.** Families moved off the
   **Gishwati–Mukura** forests now farm ~0.5 ha on steep, flood-prone resettlement sites in
   **Nyabihu, Rutsiro, Rubavu**; some, like **Laurent Hategekimana** in Nyabihu, now patrol
   as unpaid conservation auxiliaries the very forest margins their families were removed
   from. The *imidugudu* villagisation policy (14,000+ planned villages) turned domestic
   space into an expression of central planning.

8. **The coffee "reconciliation cooperative" is thirty years old — and nobody has filmed
   what it became.** **Abahuzamugambi ba Kawa** (Maraba, Huye) and **Dukunde Kawa Musasa**
   (Gakenke) were built post-1994 to put Hutu and Tutsi farmers in the same washing
   stations. The origin story is told; the present — the founders now middle-aged, their
   children working side by side — is not. A season with one or two families asks quietly
   what changed and what did not.

9. **Endangered performance and craft traditions survive through named, locatable masters.**
   The *inanga* zither has a possible last master, **Vianey Mushabizi** (Gatagara);
   **Ingoma Nshya** women drummers broke the female-drumming taboo; *imigongo* cow-dung
   geometric painting is made by genocide widows in **Kakira** (Eastern Province) and being
   commercialised; *ibyivugo* warrior poetry is being revived by living practitioners on
   **Kiruri Mountain**.

10. **Land reform's gender gap is a quiet, filmable drama.** Rwanda legislated equal land
    inheritance for women, implemented through Land Tenure Regularisation; the lived gap is
    handled case by case in legal-aid clinics run by **Haguruka** (named case: "Donatille").
    A clinic day — women arriving from surrounding villages, caseworkers translating law
    into Kinyarwanda — shows reform materialising, or failing to, in real households.

## Where to embed (named places, custodians, seasons, access)

**North — Musanze cluster (richest single base):**
- **Batwa settlements, Musanze District** (reachable by short motorbike from Musanze town);
  potters in **Kibingo**. Access via **AIMPO** and **Red Rocks Cultural Centre** (Musanze),
  which runs cultural-tourism and community programmes. Best for the ethnobotanical-knowledge
  and "Dancing Pots" angles.
- **Kinigi, Musanze** — "core location for traditional healers." Villages **Burera, Bushozi,
  Musenyi, Rugali** named in the 2021 *uburozi* study. Best for the healer-referral story and
  for **ikigage** sorghum beer (brewed nearby).
- **Amahoro Tours / Red Rocks** run beer-brewing community visits — a route into urwagwa and
  ikigage households.

**West — Gishwati slopes (Nyabihu / Rutsiro / Rubavu):**
- Resettled hillsides; sectors **Bigogwe, Jenda, Rambura** (Nyabihu). Entry via the GWLM
  socio-economic baseline survey contacts and **Laurent Hategekimana** (Nyabihu), already a
  named, quotable interlocutor.

**Centre/West — Rutsiro:**
- **Agatha Tuyisenge**, banana-beer brewer, **Mushubati Sector, Rutsiro** — a named
  protagonist with a full COVID-era story arc (documented by SENS Magazine). Follow one
  full brewing cycle toward a wedding or community gathering.

**South — coffee country (Huye / Nyamagabe / Gakenke):**
- **Maraba sector, Huye** — Cyarumbo and Sovu washing stations; **Kibingo** station (Karama
  sector, 1,300+ farmers). **Dukunde Kawa Musasa** — Musasa, Ruli, Mbilima stations
  (Gakenke), incl. women-grown-coffee groups. Harvest season for the visual core.

**Kigali + Gakenke (ritual):**
- *Kubandwa*/Ryangombe — discreet fieldwork in/around **Kigali**; academic introductions via
  **Cecilia Pennacini** and **Simon Bizimana**.
- *Umuganura* — the ritual site **Huro, Bumbogo (Gakenke)**; the national celebration falls
  the **first Friday of August**.

**Rulindo:**
- **Bernard Bizimana**, farmer growing near-vanished sorghum, millet and *isogi* — named,
  locatable, Food category.

**Eastern Province:**
- *Imigongo* widows' cooperative, **Kakira**; the **Data Centre and the Hill** geopolitics
  lead (Eastern industrial/landscape sites near recently electrified villages).

**Arts (locatable masters):**
- **Vianey Mushabizi** (inanga), **Gatagara**; **Ingoma Nshya** women drummers (Huye-based,
  internationally touring); *ibyivugo* poets, **Kiruri Mountain**.

## Partnership shape for the Goethe application

- **European partner / applicant:** INLAND (yearly publication, Italy / EU-based) — eligible
  as an EU-based cultural actor; discipline (visual arts / photography / publishing) is in
  scope for the Visual Arts strand and the Mobility grant.
- **African partner (to be confirmed — see `application/partners.md`):** a named Rwandan
  cultural organisation. Lead candidate **Red Rocks Initiatives / Red Rocks Cultural Centre
  (Musanze)** — established artist-residency and community-culture host; strong fit for an
  equitable co-creation. Alternates by theme: **AIMPO** (Batwa), **Slow Food Rwanda** (food
  heritage), **Haguruka** (women & land).
- **Two-step funding path:** a **Mobility grant** (≤€4,000) funds the EU-based INLAND
  photographer's scouting/research trip with the Rwandan partner; the **Visual Arts grant**
  funds the collaborative production (fees, travel, materials, exhibition) and a public
  presentation in Rwanda and Europe.
