# Research brief — Eidolon Grant 2026 (Vernacular / Everyday Photography)

- **Title:** Eidolon Grant 2026
- **Funder / host:** Eidolon Centre for Everyday Photography
- **Channel:** Public fund / private grant (international open call)
- **Link:** https://everydayphotography.org/centre/grant-2026
- **Deadline:** 30 September 2026, 23:59
- **Budget / award:** **€25,000 total**, split across two categories. Spendable freely on
  implementers' fees, travel, preparation and production costs, and partner payments.
- **Eligibility:** International. Open to artists, scholars, professionals, researchers,
  collectors and enthusiasts of vernacular photography whose past and proposed work centre on the
  visual heritage of everyday photography. Free entry. INLAND / a commissioned researcher-photographer eligible.

## The two categories

1. **Contemporary photography** — projects critically addressing contemporary everyday
   photographic cultures: how historical photographic traditions still shape today's society and
   visual communication, and how digital/social-media practices reshape visual narratives.
2. **Historical photography** — projects engaging 19th–20th-century everyday photography through
   private and public archives (hidden or well-known), exploring collective histories and memories.

## Fit for INLAND — score 3/5

Adjacent, needs a tailored angle. Eidolon funds *vernacular* photography — found, amateur, studio
and archival image cultures — rather than newly commissioned documentary of living custodians,
which is INLAND's core. But there is a genuine bridge: a **territory's own vernacular photographic
memory** (family albums, itinerant studio portraiture, migration and dowry photographs) is exactly
an archive of "the soul of a place before it becomes history." A well-framed Category-2 proposal
mining a target territory's everyday-photo archive would sit naturally alongside a future edition,
and €25k is real, flexible money on a free, low-burden entry.

## Candidate territory / INLAND angle

**Open — pair with a territory's vernacular photographic memory.** Strong candidates:

- **Highland Georgia / the Caucasus** (builds on Issue 02 and the W. Eugene Smith research):
  Soviet-era studio and family photography as a private archive of highland life and migration.
- **The Arbëreshë** (Albanian-speaking communities of Calabria / Basilicata / Sicily; see the
  Allianz Kulturstiftung entry): family albums and studio portraits as a diaspora-memory archive
  of an endangered-language community — everyday images of Vallja dances, weddings, laments.

Frame as memory-and-what-persists, not nostalgia: the vernacular image as the community's own
first draft of its history.
