# Photographic project / image edit — INLAND Tunisia
### Institut français · Mediterranean photography support

*The grant requires a photographic project — a **completed image edit** for the publication
strand, or a **planned shooting brief** for the production strand. Because the Tunisia edition
is at desk-research stage, this document specifies the **shooting brief and edit plan** (for the
production strand) and the **edit structure** the finished book will take (for the publication
strand). Image counts and the final selection are planning assumptions **[confirm in the
field]**.*

## What is already complete

- A full **desk-research corpus**: a deep-research report, **12 selected story leads** held to
  the INLAND three-part bar (named custodian, locatable place, seasonal scene), and a Topics &

## Shooting brief — twelve stories, three clusters

Each story is shot as a self-contained visual essay (a named protagonist, their place, and a
seasonal scene), then sequenced into the book. Indicative frames per story: a portrait set, a
process/working sequence, the place/landscape, and the object or scene the practice produces.

**The South & the Islands**
1. **The charfia of Kerkennah** ✦ — the autumn setting of the palm fronds; the dawn lift of the
   traps; the skipper's hands and face. *Season: autumn set; dawn lifts.*
2. **Djerba — Ibadi mosques & El Ghriba** — a fortified/underground mosque at prayer; a *menzel*
   courtyard and cistern; the El Ghriba Lag BaOmer procession. *Season: spring pilgrimage.*
3. **The Amazigh ksour of the Dahar** — Chenini/Douiret on the ridge; the *ghorfa* granary; an
   elder's portrait; the olive-and-barley year. *Season: olive harvest / dry months.*
4. **The seaside oasis of Gabès** — the three-tier garden; a henna grower; the old irrigation;
   the industrial edge held in one frame. *Season: henna/pomegranate cycle.*

**Cap Bon & the Sahel**
5. **The Sidi Daoud matanza** — building the net-rooms; the watch; the *matanza* haul.
   *Season: May–July.*
6. **Nabeul orange blossom** — the pre-dawn hand-picking; the copper *qattara* still; the
   bottling of *zhar* and neroli. *Season: April–May.*
7. **Mahdia wedding silk** — the draw-loom and shuttle; the gold-silk *keswa*; the dressing of a
   bride. *Season: around a real wedding.*
8. **Kairouan carpets & makroudh** — the knotting frame; dyed wool; the *makroudh* house.
   *Season: year-round.*

**The Green North**
9. **Sejnane women's pottery** — digging and kneading the clay; the open ground-firing; the
   painted dolls. *Season: dry months for firing.*
10. **Testour — Andalusian malouf** — the festival; a musician family; the backward clock and
    brick mosque. *Season: malouf festival (summer).*
11. **Stambeli (Tunis)** — a full healing night: the *gambri* and *shqasheq*, incense, the
    colours of the spirits, the trance. *Consent-led; year-round.*
12. **Aïn Draham & the Kroumirie** — the summer cork-stripping; the orange-stripped trunks;
    herding in the cork-oak forest. *Season: summer stripping.*

## Edit structure (the finished book)

- **~170–240 pages**, sequenced as twelve visual essays grouped into the three clusters, with
  the charfia recurring as the book's spine and the faith-and-memory strand (El Ghriba,
  Stambeli) threaded through.
- House style per `_inland/deliverables.md`: palette `#F9F4EE` / `#000` / accent `#400505`;
  Cormorant / Cormorant Garamond / Fraunces; long-form captions naming people and places.
- Accompanying long-form text and a short film / digital set.
