## Mediterranean-theme statement

Tunisia is one of the most layered shores of the Mediterranean: in a single small country the
sea has landed Phoenicians at Carthage, Romans, Arabs, **Andalusian Moriscos** expelled from
Spain, **Jewish** communities of great antiquity, **sub-Saharan** people brought across the
Sahara in bondage, and Ottoman, Italian and French administrators. Each arrival left a
practice that is still kept by name on the coast and the islands. *The Palm in the Sea* reads
that Mediterranean palimpsest through its living custodians — the sea as **crossing, frontier,
larder and memory** runs through every story. This is the *monde méditerranéen* the programme
exists to document, in the register INLAND already established with Issue 03, *Isole Minori*
(Italy's minor islands, 2025).

## Summary (the project in five sentences)

Tunisia is internationally legible through three loud stories — the 2011 revolution, the
Mediterranean migration crossings, and the beach-resort coast. All three are loud; all three
crowd out the quieter country. *The Palm in the Sea* documents what survived on Tunisia's
shores and islands — the practices that have been absorbed by neither the antiquity the tour
buses visit, the protectorate, the resort economy, nor the post-revolution turbulence, and can
still be witnessed and photographed today. The edition's emblem is the **charfia** of the
Kerkennah Islands: a fence of date-palm fronds planted in the sea to catch fish as it has for
centuries, inscribed by UNESCO in 2020. The result is a printed INLAND edition and a touring
photographic exhibition, made **with** the custodians, not about them.

## Artistic concept & method

INLAND's method is slow, place-based and built on trust: months of pre-production research,
then embedding a photographer (with a writer and filmmaker) with named custodians over extended
field periods. For Tunisia the desk research is advanced — **17 leads identified, 12 selected**
— each centred on a **practice that can still be witnessed, a locatable place, and a named
custodian or custodian community** (see the attached research report, selected leads, and
Topics & Contacts spreadsheet). The selection is balanced across craft, ritual, fishing, food,
language and landscape, and across the whole country, from the palm-fence fishers of Kerkennah
and the women potters of Sejnane to the Stambeli healing-trance brotherhoods of Tunis and the
cork-oak country of the Kroumirie.

Two editorial commitments shape every story: the **revolution and the migration crisis are
context, never the lens**, and the **state is never cast as villain** — the seaside oasis of
Gabès, pressed by the phosphate works at its edge, is told as the gardeners' story, tension
without indictment.

## The work (three production clusters + a signature story)

A field deployment groups the twelve storylines into three clusters that share geography and
season (see `selected-story-leads.md`):

1. **The South & the Islands** — the *charfia* of Kerkennah; Djerba's Ibadi mosques and the El
   Ghriba pilgrimage; the Amazigh ksour of the Dahar (Chenini, Douiret); the seaside oasis of
   Gabès.
2. **Cap Bon & the Sahel** — the Sidi Daoud bluefin *matanza*; the Nabeul orange-blossom
   stills; the wedding silk of Mahdia; the carpets and *makroudh* of holy Kairouan.
3. **The Green North** — the women potters of Sejnane; the Andalusian *malouf* of Testour; the
   Stambeli brotherhoods of Tunis; the Kroumirie cork-oak country above Tabarka.

A signature long-form story — **the charfia of Kerkennah** (the autumn net-setting) — runs
across the project as its recurring image, with **El Ghriba** (the spring pilgrimage) and the
**Stambeli** nights anchoring a faith-and-memory strand.

## Outputs & public presentation

- A printed INLAND Tunisia edition (~170–240 pp), retailing at €30 through INLAND's stockists.
- A touring photographic **exhibition**, proposed in Tunisia (with the Institut français de
  Tunisie **[confirm]**) and in Europe.
- A short film / digital story set.
- Custodians receive prints, credit, and a copy of the edition; community presentations in the
  places the work was made.

## Method, ethics & sustainability

Consent-led throughout; custodians are named collaborators, not subjects. Sensitive or
semi-private material (the **Stambeli** rite; the **El Ghriba** pilgrimage; family weddings in
Mahdia) is handled with explicit, revocable consent and identity protection where needed, and
is never staged for the camera. The work returns to the communities that made it through prints
and a presentation in Tunisia.

## Why the Institut français Mediterranean support

The aid is **open to professional photographers/publishers who have already exhibited or
published, regardless of nationality**, and is **theme-locked to the Mediterranean** — both
conditions INLAND meets (see `track-record.md`). It covers exactly INLAND's cost centres: the
**publication** strand funds the editing and printing of the finished edition; the
**production** strand funds the field shoot for the new Mediterranean photographic work. See
`budget.md` and `timeline.md`.
