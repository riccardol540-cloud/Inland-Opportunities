# Research brief — Pulitzer Center, Global Reporting Grants

- **Title**: Pulitzer Center — Global Reporting Grants (underreported stories)
- **Funder / host**: Pulitzer Center on Crisis Reporting
- **Channel**: Public fund (reporting grant)
- **Link**: https://pulitzercenter.org/grants-fellowships/opportunities-journalists/global-reporting-grants
- **Deadline**: **Rolling — no deadline**; grants are awarded on a rolling basis. The date on the card is a nominal placeholder, not a cut-off. Not URGENT.
- **Budget / award**: Most awards for international travel are **US$5,000–10,000** (may be more or less depending on circumstances).
- **Eligibility (why INLAND qualifies)**: Open worldwide to **staff and freelance journalists**; supports projects across all media (print, photography, audio, video) and encourages ambitious multi-format proposals. INLAND applies **via its commissioned photographer/writer**, with the required **letter(s) of interest/commitment from a publishing outlet** and a distribution plan.

## Fit — 3 (Possible)

This is the Center's topic-open "catch-all" grant: no restriction on subject or location, all media welcome, and an explicit appetite for **in-depth reporting on critical but overlooked issues**. An INLAND field body of work fits *if* it is framed as an under-reported cultural-continuity story with a named outlet home. Two things keep it at 3 rather than 4: the **journalism register** (reporting, not the slow anthropological essay) and the hard requirement for an **outlet distribution commitment**, which INLAND must arrange up front.

## Possible territory / angle

**Open** — pairs best with a territory whose living-tradition story is genuinely under-reported and can carry an outlet. Lead candidate: **Armenia** (an INLAND research edition):

- **Lake Sevan** fishing cultures and the freshwater ecology that shapes them (human–nature theme; seasonal).
- **Duduk and the lament / oral tradition** as a women's-and-community archive — named tradition-bearers, locatable villages.
- The **cultural afterlife of displacement** (post-2020 Nagorno-Karabakh) kept strictly as background context, never the lens — continuity and return, not the conflict.

Framed as "what survived, and can still be witnessed," with a partnering outlet lined up for distribution.

## Required application documents (for the Producer)

- Reporting proposal (topic, location, angle)
- Distribution plan + letter(s) of interest/commitment from outlet(s)
- Budget (travel and production)
- Journalist CV / portfolio
