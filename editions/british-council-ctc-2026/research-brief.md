# Research brief — British Council: Connections Through Culture Grants 2026

- **Title:** Connections Through Culture (CTC) Grants 2026
- **Funder / host:** British Council (Arts)
- **Channel:** partnership / co-production
- **Link:** https://arts.britishcouncil.org/connections-through-culture · call: https://on-the-move.org/news/british-council-connections-through-culture-grants-2026
- **Deadline:** 2026-08-12 (23:59 BST) — **URGENT** (≈3 weeks). Applications opened 17 Jun 2026.
- **Award:** £5,000–£15,000 per country partnership (varies by partner country).

## Eligibility (why INLAND qualifies)
Grants seed **new cross-border collaborations** between at least one **UK-based** partner and at least one partner in an eligible participating country. **Italy is a participating country**, so INLAND (Italy) is eligible **provided it teams with a UK cultural partner** (a UK publisher, photography institution, university, or museum). Each partner must be legally based in its own country. Non-audiovisual cultural and creative sectors are in scope — **visual arts, photography, heritage and publishing** all qualify.

## Fit — 3/5
A genuine **relationship / co-production foothold**, not edition-production money. The budget is small and the collaboration must be UK-anchored, which is why it sits at 3 rather than higher. But it is squarely a partnership-channel opportunity (the third agreed channel), it is **open now with a near deadline**, and it is a low-burden way to seed a UK–Italy INLAND collaboration — e.g. co-developing an edition's research or a UK exhibition/publication partner for a future issue.

## Possible territory / angle
The collaboration is UK↔Italy, but the *subject* can be any INLAND territory. Strongest use: pair with a UK partner (photography gallery, book publisher, or an area-studies department) to co-develop research or a UK launch for an in-progress edition, or to build the UK leg of a future Italy-anchored edition (e.g. the Arbëreshë / interior-Italy threads already on the board).

## Required application documents
- Project concept (collaboration aims, cross-border rationale)
- Confirmed UK partner + eligible-country partner (both legally based in their countries)
- Simple budget (£5,000–£15,000 band) and activity plan
- Applicant/partner profiles
